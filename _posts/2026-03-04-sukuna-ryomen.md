## Introduction

Ryomen Sukuna (両面宿儺) is a legendary figure in **Japanese folklore** long before his appearance in “*Jujutsu Kaisen*”. In classical tales, Sukuna was a fearsome warrior spirit with two faces and four arms, feared for his immense strength and ferocity.

In the modern manga/anime *Jujutsu Kaisen* (呪術廻戦), written by **芥見下々 (Gege Akutami)**, Sukuna is reimagined as the **King of Curses**, an A‑Grade special grade cursed spirit whose strength surpasses even the most skilled sorcerers.

---

## Origins in Japanese Myth

The character **Sukuna (宿儺)** first appears in ancient Japanese literature, such as the *Nihon Shoki* (日本書紀) and *Kojiki* (古事記), where he is described as:

- A powerful supernatural being
- Dual‑faced and multi‑limbed
- Extremely dangerous and violent

These earliest references depict him as more monster than man, setting the stage for his adaptation in modern storytelling.

---

## Sukuna in *Jujutsu Kaisen*

In *Jujutsu Kaisen*, Sukuna (宿儺) is a legendary curse born over 1,000 years ago whose power was so great that humans could not control or seal him, leading to the creation of his fingers as **special grade cursed objects**.

When all 20 of Sukuna’s fingers are consumed, he is said to return to full strength.

### Personality and Traits

Ryomen Sukuna is:

✔ Arrogant and merciless  
✔ Ruthlessly strategic  
✔ Highly intelligent  
✔ Obsessed with strength and domination  

Even seasoned sorcerers tremble at his name.

---

## Powers and Abilities

Sukuna’s abilities in *Jujutsu Kaisen* include:

### ✦ Cursed Energy Mastery
He wields vast amounts of cursed energy capable of devastating curses and sorcerers alike.

### ✦ Dismantle & Cleave
Signature slashing techniques that can cut through anything.

### ✦ Domain Expansion — Malevolent Shrine  
A unique Domain with precision and unmatched lethality.

### ✦ Reverse Cursed Technique  
Capable of healing himself and potentially others.

These abilities make Sukuna the most feared entity in the *Jujutsu Kaisen* universe.

---

## Sukuna’s Influence on the Story

Even while sealed within **Yuji Itadori**, Sukuna remains a pivotal force:

- Frequently interfering in battles
- Manipulating allies and enemies
- Seeking ways to regain full power

His presence drives plot and tension throughout the series.

---

## Japanese Cultural Significance

Sukuna’s roots in Japanese myth reflect the historical fear and reverence of “cursed spirits” (呪霊). Ancient texts treated such beings as:

- Natural disasters
- Spiritual punishments
- Embodiments of human fear

This cultural background enriches his portrayal in *Jujutsu Kaisen*, tying fiction to traditional spiritual themes.

---

## Legacy

Ryomen Sukuna has become one of the most iconic figures in *Jujutsu Kaisen* — admired and feared by fans worldwide.

Whether viewed as folklore or anime villain, Sukuna’s presence resonates with the timeless concept of fear itself.
